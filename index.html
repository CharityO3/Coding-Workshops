The German Shepherd, also known in Britain as an Alsatian, is a German breed of working dog of medium to large size. The breed was developed by Max von Stephanitz using various traditional German herding dogs from 1899. It was originally bred as a herding dog, for herding sheep. Learn more about the German Shepard on Wikipedia.

German Shepherds are known for their protective instincts, as they often bark at any unfamiliar sounds or faces. Raising one is not without its challenges, as you might need to invest time and effort into socializing it with other dogs and people. Seeking the assistance of a professional dog trainer to address any behavioral issues and ensure a well-rounded, well-behaved dog is highly advisable. See more images of the German Shepard.
